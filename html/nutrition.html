Food for Thought

Good nutrition is a lifestyle, not a diet. It starts with a new way of thinking about food. Think of your body as a vehicle that uses food for fuel. Fill your tank with the right type of fuel. Fill your tank with the right type of fuel, and you will have more energy, feel better, and perform better.

Nutrition is the process of taking in food and using it for growth, metabolism, and repair. It is essential for maintaining health and well-being.

Nutrition is not just about eating the right foods, but also about understanding how those foods affect your body and how to make healthy choices.

Good nutrition is important for everyone, but it is especially important for athletes and those who are physically active. Proper nutrition can help improve performance, reduce the risk of injury, and aid in recovery.

What to eat

Use the following as a guideline:

  • Fruits and vegetables: 5 servings a day
  • Whole grains: 3 servings a day
  • Lean proteins: 2 servings a day
  • Dairy: 2 servings a day
  • Healthy fats: in moderation

Limit the consumption of bread, pasta, white potatoes, and processed food. These foods are high in carbohydrates

Meal of the Week

Herb Roasted Chicken

Ingredients:

  • 1 whole chicken
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 tablespoon rosemary
  • 1/4 tablespoon thyme
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

Combine all ingredients in a plastic bag and marinate for at leats an hour.
